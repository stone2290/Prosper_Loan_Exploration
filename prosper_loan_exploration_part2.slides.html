Effects of Borrower Characteristics on The Interest Rate From Prosper¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this ivestigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of borrowers that affecting their borrowe interest rate from Prosper. The main focus was on the nine variables: BorrowerRate, CreditGrade, Term, LenderYield, ProsperScore, EmploymentStatus, IsBorrowerHomeowner, CreditScoreRangeLower", CreditScoreRangeUpper, DebtToIncomeRatio, LoanOriginationDate.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. The attributes included nine variables as mention above. Sixty-eight columns were removed from the analysis due to inconsistencies or missing information.

Distribution of Borrower Rate¶

The distribution of borrower rate is roughly an unimodal shape and it is roughly a right skew shape. The number of borrowers with the interest rate of approximately 0.32 is the largest, followed by 0.15.

Distribution of Average Credit Score¶

Credit score is a measure of the borrower's ability to repay the loan. I decided to use the average credit score instead of two variables (CreditScoreRangeUpper and CreditScoreRangeLower). The average credit score distribution is roughly unimodal, and there are more than 700 records. There are few records near 600, so the shape is not so smooth.

Borrower Rate Vs. Average Credit Score¶

The higher the average credit score, the lower the borrower's interest rate.In the range of 400 to 1000 credit scores, there is indeed a negative linear relationship between the average credit score and the borrower’s interest rate.

Borrower Rate and Average Credit Score by Year¶

The slope of the average credit score and the borrower rate became steeper as the year increased, which meant that the borrower’s interest rate was increasingly sensitive to changes in credit score.

Borrower rate and average credit score by term¶

The longer the term, the more concentrated the loan rate and average credit score. This is reasonable because long-term interest rates are usually more stable than short-term interest rates.
